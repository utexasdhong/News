# Health & Medicine — March 2026

## 1. [FDA approves Awiqli, first once-weekly basal insulin for type 2 diabetes](https://www.pulmonologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** Pulmonology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-03-26

Why this matters: Insulin icodec-abae (Novo Nordisk) becomes the first once-weekly basal insulin approved in the US for adults with type 2 diabetes, replacing daily injections. For the ~30M Americans with type 2 diabetes who use basal insulin, this is the biggest adherence/quality-of-life change in decades — and likely a multi-billion-dollar commercial reset.

## 2. [FDA approves Icotyde (icotrokinra), oral IL-23 antagonist for plaque psoriasis](https://www.pulmonologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** Pulmonology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-03-18

Why this matters: Icotrokinra is an oral IL-23 receptor antagonist — meaning psoriasis patients can take a pill instead of an injectable biologic. If real-world efficacy holds, it's a significant disruption of the injectable-biologic dermatology franchise (Skyrizi, Tremfya, Stelara).

## 3. [FDA approves Lynavoy (linerixibat) for cholestatic pruritus in PBC](https://www.pulmonologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** Pulmonology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-03-19

Why this matters: Linerixibat (GSK) addresses one of the most distressing symptoms of primary biliary cholangitis — debilitating itch. A targeted symptom-relief approval for a rare-disease population that previously had limited options.

## 4. [FDA accelerated approval of Yuviwel for childhood achondroplasia](https://www.pulmonologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** Pulmonology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-02-27 (in-month news cycle through March)

Why this matters: Navepegritide, a C-type natriuretic peptide analog, becomes the second-ever approved therapy for achondroplasia in children. Pediatric rare-disease approvals at this pace mark a real shift in FDA's willingness to use accelerated pathways post-IRA pricing reforms.

## 5. [FDA approves Ponlimsi (denosumab-adet), Prolia biosimilar for osteoporosis](https://www.pulmonologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** Pulmonology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-03-31

Why this matters: A RANKL-inhibitor biosimilar to Amgen's Prolia — one of the highest-volume osteoporosis drugs globally. Biosimilar entry typically drives 30-50% price cuts in 18 months, a material out-of-pocket-cost story for tens of millions of older Americans.
