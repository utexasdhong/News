# Crypto & Fintech — March 2026

## 1. [20 millionth Bitcoin mined — only 1M BTC left to issue over the next 114 years](https://cryptal.com/en/blog/bitcoin-price-march-2026-analysis)
**Source:** Cryptal · **Date:** 2026-03-10

Why this matters: A symbolic supply milestone — 95.2% of the eventual 21M total supply is now in circulation, and the remaining 1M will trickle out over more than a century. Reinforces Bitcoin's hard-cap narrative just as the institutional adoption story (ETFs, sovereign reserves) matures, and is one of the cleanest "scarcity" data points the asset will ever produce.

## 2. [SEC + CFTC announce unprecedented joint crypto-oversight collaboration](https://www.oanda.com/us-en/trade-tap-blog/asset-classes/crypto/latest-crypto-developments-in-march-2026/)
**Source:** OANDA · **Date:** 2026-03-06

Why this matters: The two US regulators that have spent a decade in jurisdictional turf war announced a coordinated approach to digital-asset oversight. Industry read it as the strongest signal yet that a unified federal framework is now possible — particularly with stablecoin legislation already advancing.

## 3. [Bitcoin tests $72-74K then retreats post-FOMC](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/03/25/bitcoin-nears-usd72-000-as-rising-open-interest-signals-growing-leverage-in-choppy-market)
**Source:** CoinDesk · **Date:** 2026-03-25

Why this matters: BTC peaked near $74K, dropped to $70.5K post-FOMC March 18 ("sell the news"), then drifted around $69-72K with elevated open interest signaling growing leverage. A textbook leverage-driven choppy market in a year that started with BTC down ~20% — the institutional adoption story isn't translating into one-way price action.

## 4. [Stablecoin supply crosses $273B, real-world payments volume doubles to $400B](https://www.fintechweekly.com/news/stablecoin-predictions-2026-payments-infrastructure-regulation)
**Source:** FinTech Weekly · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Stablecoins are no longer "crypto plumbing" — 60% of real-world stablecoin payment volume is now B2B, and 2025 adjusted volumes ($10.9T) approached Visa's annual payments volume ($14.2T). The infrastructure story is now bigger than the speculation story.

## 5. [Tillis-Alsobrooks agreement breaks CLARITY Act stablecoin-yield deadlock](https://www.fintechweekly.com/news/stablecoin-predictions-2026-payments-infrastructure-regulation)
**Source:** FinTech Weekly · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Senators Tillis (R) and Alsobrooks (D) reached an in-principle deal on the stablecoin-yield question that had stalled the CLARITY Act since January. With this unblocked, the first comprehensive US crypto market-structure law has a realistic 2026 path — which would close the most significant regulatory gap left in US financial services.
