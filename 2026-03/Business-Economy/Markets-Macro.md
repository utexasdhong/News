# Markets & Macro — March 2026

## 1. [FOMC holds rates at 3.5-3.75%, raises inflation forecast for 2026-27](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260318.htm)
**Source:** Federal Reserve · **Date:** 2026-03-18

Why this matters: The Fed left rates unchanged with one dissent (Gov. Miran for a cut). Core PCE forecast was revised up to 2.7% for 2026 and 2.2% for 2027; GDP growth nudged up to 2.4%/2.3%/2.1% across 2026-28. The committee acknowledged Middle East uncertainty in its statement — formal recognition that the Iran war is a Fed-relevant variable.

## 2. [Trump invokes Section 122 for new 10-15% global tariffs after Supreme Court blocks IEEPA](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/stocks-drop-after-trump-ramps-up-new-tariffs-and-investors-dump-potential-ai-losers)
**Source:** PBS NewsHour · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: After SCOTUS struck down the IEEPA-based tariffs as exceeding presidential authority, Trump pivoted within a day to Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 — a 10% global tariff escalated to 15%. The S&P 500 fell 1% and the Dow dropped 822 points on the announcement. The legal-vehicle game (rather than the tariff debate itself) is now the dominant trade story.

## 3. [March PCE inflation reading lands above Fed target](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/pce-inflation-rate-march-2026.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-03 (reported 2026-04-30)

Why this matters: Headline and core PCE for March (released April 30) confirmed the upward inflation revision the FOMC made mid-month. With tariffs flowing through to goods prices and oil elevated by the Iran war, the "transitory" framing is dead — the Fed's hold-at-3.5-3.75% posture is the new baseline through summer at minimum.

## 4. [S&P 500 trades sideways YTD as rest-of-world equities outperform](https://www.usbank.com/investing/financial-perspectives/market-news/stock-market-under-trump.html)
**Source:** U.S. Bank · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: With the S&P 500 flat year-to-date and global ex-US markets up ~10%, the multi-year US equity outperformance has visibly broken. CAPE ratio above 40 in January remains historically associated with subsequent 30% drawdowns. The "American exceptionalism" trade is at its weakest since 2017.

## 5. [Energy prices climb on Iran war and Strait of Hormuz closure threat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Iran's IRGC declared the Strait of Hormuz closed; attacks on Gulf oil and gas infrastructure pushed global energy prices up materially through March. About 20% of global oil flows through Hormuz — even a partial disruption changes the inflation arithmetic for every central bank.
