# International Politics — March 2026

## 1. [Mojtaba Khamenei named Iran's Supreme Leader after father's assassination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Following Ali Khamenei's death in a joint US-Israeli strike (Feb 28), Iran installed Mojtaba Khamenei — a dynastic succession that resolves the immediate leadership vacuum but cements a war footing. Iran's IRGC also declared the Strait of Hormuz "closed," instantly making this the most consequential geopolitical story of the month.

## 2. [Nepal's Rastriya Swatantra Party wins post-protest election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03-05

Why this matters: The centrist RSP wins most seats in a snap election triggered by anti-corruption youth protests. South Asia's pattern of "youth-led mass mobilization replaces incumbent" continues (Bangladesh 2024, Sri Lanka 2022), and Nepal's outcome is the cleanest electoral resolution of the model to date.

## 3. [Denmark's general election ends with no majority bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03-24

Why this matters: Neither left nor right bloc reached a majority — a Scandinavian hung parliament that will likely require unconventional coalition arithmetic. Denmark has been the EU's hawkish voice on defense spending and Russia; the post-election government's composition will move European defense posture by several degrees in either direction.

## 4. [France's first-round municipal elections boost right and centrist parties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: The 2026 French municipal elections are the last national barometer before the 2027 presidential race. Rassemblement National-aligned gains in mayoral contests are the clearest indicator yet that the far-right is consolidating below the national level — the ground game that wins presidencies.

## 5. [Cuba enters direct embargo talks with the Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03-13

Why this matters: President Miguel Díaz-Canel opened formal negotiations to end the US embargo — the first substantive bilateral engagement in years. With Maduro in US custody after January's Venezuela operation, the Trump administration is reshuffling the entire Latin America policy chessboard, and Cuba is now a piece in play.
