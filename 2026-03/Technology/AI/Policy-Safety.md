# Policy & Safety — March 2026

## 1. [White House releases National Policy Framework for AI](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/white-house-releases-a-national-policy-framework-for-artificial)
**Source:** Holland & Knight · **Date:** 2026-03-20

Why this matters: Non-binding legislative recommendations covering seven priority areas — child safety, economic growth, innovation, workforce, and federal preemption of state laws. The headline policy choice is preemption: the Trump administration wants Congress to override the patchwork of state AI laws with one minimal federal standard, a structural fight that will play out across 2026.

## 2. [New York amends RAISE Act toward transparency-based framework](https://www.cooley.com/news/insight/2026/2026-04-24-state-ai-laws-where-are-they-now)
**Source:** Cooley · **Date:** 2026-03-27

Why this matters: Governor Hochul shifted the Responsible AI Safety and Education Act from a heavier safety-evaluation regime toward disclosure/reporting. New York is the most consequential state in the AI-policy debate after California, and a lighter-touch revision there foreshadows where compromise legislation could land federally.

## 3. [California EO directs AI safety requirements for state-vendor companies](https://www.cooley.com/news/insight/2026/2026-04-24-state-ai-laws-where-are-they-now)
**Source:** Cooley · **Date:** 2026-03-30

Why this matters: Newsom's Executive Order N-5-26 routes around the legislature by using state procurement as leverage — any company doing business with California state agencies will face AI safety requirements. Procurement-as-regulation is now the live mechanism states use when bills stall.

## 4. [EU publishes second draft of AI-content labelling Code of Practice](https://epthinktank.eu/2026/03/18/enforcement-of-the-ai-act/)
**Source:** European Parliament Think Tank · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: The Commission's second draft refines marking and labelling rules for AI-generated content under the AI Act, with finalization targeted for mid-2026. With GPAI rules already enforceable since August 2025 and the Commission's full enforcement powers kicking in August 2, 2026, every frontier lab is now operating under EU oversight in production.

## 5. [White House framework explicitly recommends preempting state AI laws](https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/260402-trump-administration-releases-national-ai-policy-framework)
**Source:** Morrison Foerster · **Date:** 2026-03-20

Why this matters: The most controversial framework recommendation is preemption: Congress is asked to override "undue" state burdens with a single national standard. Democrats are publicly skeptical on accountability and oversight grounds — this is the policy fault line that will define 2026 AI legislation.
