# Markets & Macro — April 2026

## 1. ["Liberation Day" tariffs trigger the worst US market days since the 2020 crash; Trump pauses on April 9](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/the-u-s-stock-market-had-a-rocky-start-to-the-year-thanks-to-tariffs-and-trumps-fight-with-the-fed-but-is-ending-on-a-high-note)
**Source:** PBS NewsHour · **Date:** 2026-04-03 to 2026-04-09

Why this matters: April 3: S&P 500 down nearly 5% on the broader-than-expected tariff announcement. April 4: another 6% drop on China's retaliation. April 9: tariff pause announced explicitly citing bond-market stress. The episode formally inverted the political pressure point — bonds, not equities, are the binding constraint on Trump tariff policy. By mid-month, S&P 500 and Nasdaq were back at all-time highs.

## 2. [Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%; minutes flag tariff + energy as two-sided risk](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260429.htm)
**Source:** Federal Reserve · **Date:** 2026-04-29

Why this matters: April FOMC kept the funds rate at 3.50–3.75% with inflation elevated, energy prices spiking on the Iran war, and labor markets stabilizing. Minutes show several participants seeing rate cuts later in 2026 if tariff effects fade — but others worried about embedded inflation. The committee is more cautious than markets have priced.

## 3. [S&P 500 and Nasdaq hit fresh all-time highs mid-April after tariff pause](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/14/stock-market-today-live-updates.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-04-15

Why this matters: S&P 500 closed at 7,022.95 and Nasdaq at 24,016.02 on April 15 — record highs reached just over a week after the worst two-day decline of the year. The whiplash defines the regime: macro driven by political headlines and policy reversals more than by earnings or rates.

## 4. [IMF flags war shock; revises 2026 global growth to 3.1% with inflation at 4.4%](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/weo/issues/2026/04/14/world-economic-outlook-april-2026)
**Source:** IMF World Economic Outlook (April 2026) · **Date:** 2026-04-14

Why this matters: The IMF's April WEO is built around the Iran war and the Strait of Hormuz disruption. Assuming a short-lived conflict and a moderate 19% energy-price increase, global growth slips to 3.1% in 2026 and headline inflation runs at 4.4%. The downside scenarios are materially worse. The IMF is openly building cases for stagflation policy responses.

## 5. [Yale Budget Lab puts the applied effective US tariff rate at 7–8.5% post-changes](https://budgetlab.yale.edu/research/state-us-tariffs-april-8-2026)
**Source:** Yale Budget Lab · **Date:** 2026-04-08

Why this matters: After the April 9 pauses, Yale Budget Lab estimates the effective applied US tariff rate settles around 7–8.5% — the highest since the 1940s. Materially raises the price level of imported goods (CBO and Fed analysis both flag persistence) and reshapes the supply-chain calculus for every manufacturer importing into the US.
