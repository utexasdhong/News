# International Politics — April 2026

## 1. [Péter Magyar's Tisza party unseats Viktor Orbán in Hungary's parliamentary election](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/12/world/live-news/hungary-election-orban-magyar)
**Source:** CNN · **Date:** 2026-04-12

Why this matters: Tisza took 138 of 199 seats on 53.6% of the vote against Fidesz's 55 seats and 37.8%. Nearly 80% turnout, two-thirds majority — enough to amend the constitution. The most consequential European political event of the month: it likely ends Hungary's adversarial posture inside the EU, potentially unblocking a €90 billion loan to Ukraine that Orbán had vetoed.

## 2. [Iran-Israel-US conflict ceasefire (April 8) and Lebanon ceasefire (April 16)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_the_2026_Iran_war)
**Source:** Wikipedia (Timeline of the 2026 Iran war) · **Date:** 2026-04-08 / 2026-04-16

Why this matters: The conditional ceasefire ended 40 days of major combat begun February 28; a separate Israel-Lebanon ceasefire took hold April 16 but Hizballah skirmishing continued in the south. The April 13 US counter-blockade of the Strait of Hormuz kept oil markets unsettled. The shape of the post-war regional order — Iranian succession, Hizballah's standing, Gulf realignment — is the dominant 2026 geopolitical story.

## 3. [Bulgarian parliamentary election: Rumen Radev's Progressive Bulgaria wins landslide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_national_electoral_calendar)
**Source:** Wikipedia (2026 national electoral calendar) · **Date:** 2026-04-19

Why this matters: Former president Radev's new Progressive Bulgaria vehicle won a parliamentary landslide just months after Bulgaria joined the eurozone (January 1). Combined with Hungary's flip, two Central/Eastern European EU members realigned politically in a single quarter — a significant rebalancing in the EU's eastern flank.

## 4. [Peru holds first round of general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_national_electoral_calendar)
**Source:** Wikipedia (2026 national electoral calendar) · **Date:** 2026-04-12 to 2026-04-13

Why this matters: Peru's election cycle takes place against deep political fragmentation, ongoing protests, and the post-Boluarte transition. A second round follows in June. Markets watch Peru closely as Latin America's second-largest copper producer — leadership outcomes affect mining royalty policy globally.

## 5. [JD Vance travels to Hungary to back Orbán just before his defeat](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/12/hungary-election-early-results-show-magyars-tisza-ahead-of-orbans-fidesz)
**Source:** Al Jazeera · **Date:** 2026-04-07

Why this matters: The VP's April 7 visit to Budapest in support of Orbán — followed five days later by Orbán's landslide loss — is a high-profile data point on the limits of MAGA-style endorsements to swing European elections, and on the awkward gap between US executive-branch alignment and actual European political momentum.
