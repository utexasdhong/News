# Notable Culture — April 2026

## 1. ["Michael" biopic opens, grosses over $600M to become #2 music biopic worldwide](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Major-Events-of-2026)
**Source:** Britannica (Major Events of 2026) · **Date:** 2026-04-24

Why this matters: Antoine Fuqua's "Michael," covering Michael Jackson's early career with Jaafar Jackson in the lead, opened April 24 and grossed over $600M globally within weeks — second only to "Bohemian Rhapsody" among music biopics. Validates the music-biopic-as-tentpole model and reignites the cultural conversation about Jackson's legacy and the documentary record.

## 2. [Afrika Bambaataa dies at 67](https://www.cbsnews.com/pictures/notable-deaths-in-2026/)
**Source:** CBS News · **Date:** 2026-04-10

Why this matters: One of hip-hop's three founding DJs alongside Kool Herc and Grandmaster Flash, Bambaataa's 1980s electro records ("Planet Rock," "Looking for the Perfect Beat") are foundational to a half-century of dance and hip-hop production. The Zulu Nation's role in shaping early hip-hop culture remains his lasting institutional legacy, alongside complicated late-career allegations that the obituaries had to address.

## 3. [Asha Bhosle dies — Indian playback legend with over 12,000 recorded songs](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Deaths-in-2026)
**Source:** Britannica (Deaths in 2026) · **Date:** 2026-04-19

Why this matters: Across eight decades and 20 Indian languages, Bhosle recorded what is widely considered the largest catalog of any vocalist in history. Her sister Lata Mangeshkar's 2022 death and Bhosle's passing close out one of the most consequential creative dynasties in 20th-century popular music.

## 4. [Michael Tilson Thomas, longtime San Francisco Symphony music director, dies](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Deaths-in-2026)
**Source:** Britannica (Deaths in 2026) · **Date:** 2026-04-22

Why this matters: MTT shaped the San Francisco Symphony into one of America's defining orchestras, championing American composers (Copland, Ives, Bernstein) and pioneering classical-music broadcasting through "Keeping Score." A central figure in late-20th and early-21st century American classical music.

## 5. [Prince estate hosts five-day "Celebration of Life" festival at Paisley Park](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Major-Events-of-2026)
**Source:** Britannica (Major Events of 2026) · **Date:** 2026-04-21

Why this matters: Marking 10 years since Prince's death, the multi-venue festival across Paisley Park, Minneapolis, and St. Paul drew global pilgrimage. Anchors continued growth in destination music tourism (Graceland, Abbey Road, Paisley Park) and is part of the Prince estate's ongoing brand-and-vault strategy that has reshaped post-mortem catalog management.
