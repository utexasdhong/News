# Major World Events — April 2026

## 1. [Iran war ceasefire on April 8 ends 40 days of US-Israel-Iran combat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_the_2026_Iran_war)
**Source:** Wikipedia (Timeline of the 2026 Iran war) · **Date:** 2026-04-08

Why this matters: The single most consequential geopolitical event of the month. After 40 days of combat begun by the February 28 strikes, a conditional ceasefire took effect April 8. The US then launched a counter-blockade of the Strait of Hormuz on April 13, keeping global energy markets in crisis even after the shooting stopped. UN Secretary-General Guterres called it the worst energy-security threat in history.

## 2. [Péter Magyar unseats Viktor Orbán in Hungary's landslide parliamentary election](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/12/world/live-news/hungary-election-orban-magyar)
**Source:** CNN · **Date:** 2026-04-12

Why this matters: 16 years of Orbán government ended in a single night. Tisza took 138 of 199 seats — a two-thirds majority — on nearly 80% turnout. Likely unblocks the €90 billion EU loan to Ukraine that Orbán had vetoed, and ends Hungary's role as the EU's internal spoiler on Russia, rule-of-law conditionality, and migration policy.

## 3. [Trump's "Liberation Day" tariffs roil global markets, paused April 9](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/the-u-s-stock-market-had-a-rocky-start-to-the-year-thanks-to-tariffs-and-trumps-fight-with-the-fed-but-is-ending-on-a-high-note)
**Source:** PBS NewsHour · **Date:** 2026-04-03 to 2026-04-09

Why this matters: The April 3 tariff package triggered two-day declines (≈5% then 6%) that were the worst since the 2020 COVID crash. Trump paused tariffs April 9 citing bond-market stress. The episode redefined how political markets read trade policy — and the IMF rewrote its April WEO around tariff-plus-war stagflation risk.

## 4. [Sudan's civil war enters its fourth year; 33.7 million need humanitarian aid](https://www.rescue.org/article/top-10-crises-world-cant-ignore-2026)
**Source:** International Rescue Committee · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: Sudan remains the world's largest displacement crisis and one of the worst hunger emergencies in modern history. The conflict between SAF and RSF reached its fourth-year mark in April with no political off-ramp and chronic underfunding of the UN appeal. Persistent, structural, under-covered.

## 5. [IMF April WEO: global growth marked down to 3.1% amid "shadow of war"](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/weo/issues/2026/04/14/world-economic-outlook-april-2026)
**Source:** IMF · **Date:** 2026-04-14

Why this matters: The IMF's April 14 World Economic Outlook is titled "Global Economy in the Shadow of War." Headline forecast: 3.1% global growth, 4.4% inflation, conditional on a short conflict and only moderate energy-price increases. The Fund is openly signaling stagflation as the central scenario for 2026 — a meaningful shift from January's outlook.
