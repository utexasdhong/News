# Hardware & Chips — April 2026

## 1. [Nvidia locks up TSMC CoWoS packaging capacity; Intel and TSMC ramp US plants](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/tsmc-nvidia-advanced-packaging-intel.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-04-08

Why this matters: Nvidia has reserved the majority of TSMC's leading-edge CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity — the bottleneck for every high-end AI accelerator. TSMC is so booked it's outsourcing simpler packaging steps to ASE and Amkor. Meanwhile both TSMC (Arizona) and Intel are ramping US packaging facilities. Advanced packaging — not wafers — is now the constraint that decides who ships AI chips on time.

## 2. [Musk taps Intel to package custom chips for SpaceX, xAI and Tesla at Terafab in Texas](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/tsmc-nvidia-advanced-packaging-intel.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: Musk routing his three companies' custom-silicon packaging through Intel — at his planned Terafab in Texas — is a meaningful win for Intel Foundry and a vote that the Pat Gelsinger-era foundry strategy still has commercial pull. It also gives Musk a hedge against TSMC capacity rationing.

## 3. [Approximately 133 companies now actively developing or selling AI chips](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260407PD205/ai-chip-tsmc-chips-nvidia-amd.html)
**Source:** DigiTimes (citing SEMIEcosystem / Jon Peddie Research) · **Date:** 2026-04-07

Why this matters: A SEMIEcosystem report puts the AI-silicon vendor count at ~133 — startups, hyperscaler internal silicon (TPU, Trainium, Maia), incumbents, and Chinese alternatives. The market is bifurcating: Nvidia still owns the high end, but the long tail of inference-specific silicon is finally large enough to pressure margins.

## 4. [Nvidia's CES platform of six TSMC-made chips ships through April](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2026/01/07/2003850159)
**Source:** Taipei Times · **Date:** 2026-04 (platform announced CES, shipping ramps through Q2)

Why this matters: The six-chip Vera Rubin platform announced at CES — built on TSMC's 3nm and 5nm nodes — entered volume shipment in April, anchoring data-center buildouts at Meta, Microsoft, Google, and OpenAI's Stargate partners. The Hopper-to-Blackwell-to-Rubin generational pace is now roughly one year, locking in Nvidia's compounding lead.

## 5. [Global AI chip ecosystem competition intensifies; TSMC remains the linchpin](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260407PD205/ai-chip-tsmc-chips-nvidia-amd.html)
**Source:** DigiTimes · **Date:** 2026-04-07

Why this matters: AMD's MI400 series, Intel's Gaudi 4, and Chinese alternatives (Huawei Ascend) all gained traction in April announcements — but every one still depends on TSMC for leading-edge wafers or packaging. Geographic concentration of advanced node manufacturing in Taiwan remains the single biggest strategic risk in the global tech stack.
