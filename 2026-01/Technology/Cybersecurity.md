# Cybersecurity — January 2026

## 1. [Eurail confirms breach, 1.3TB of customer data offered for sale](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/major-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-in-january-2026)
**Source:** CM Alliance · **Date:** 2026-01-10

Why this matters: Travel companion details and passport information were among the data leaked. It hits at a uniquely sensitive intersection — identity documents plus travel patterns — and Eurail's pan-European customer base makes this a continent-wide incident, not a national one.

## 2. [Frost Bank hit by Everest ransomware, ~250K customers exposed](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/major-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-in-january-2026)
**Source:** CM Alliance · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: US regional banks have been a soft target for ransomware groups; Frost is one of the largest hits this year. Banking breaches are where regulators and class-action plaintiffs both pile on — the long tail of cost will dwarf the ransom decision.

## 3. [European Space Agency: 200GB of source code, API tokens, Bitbucket repos stolen](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/major-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-in-january-2026)
**Source:** CM Alliance · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Source code and active API tokens from a major space agency is a supply-chain risk waiting to be exploited. Anything ESA's collaborative engineering systems touched — partners, contractors, downstream space programs — is potentially also compromised.

## 4. [Brightspeed telecom: Crimson Collective claims >1M customer records](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/major-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-in-january-2026)
**Source:** CM Alliance · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: A new aggressive extortion group hit a US telecom at million-record scale. Telecom data — phone numbers, account details, billing — is gold for downstream SIM-swap and account-takeover attacks; the impact will play out for months.

## 5. [Qilin emerges as the most prolific ransomware group of January](https://www.blackfog.com/the-state-of-ransomware-2026/)
**Source:** BlackFog · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Qilin (also tracked as Agenda) hit a US Airport Authority and a Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturer — sectors with national-security overtones. Ransomware attacks overall surged ~30% vs. the prior nine-month average. The threat landscape isn't stabilizing; it's accelerating.
