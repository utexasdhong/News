# Hardware & Chips — January 2026

## 1. [NVIDIA unveils Vera Rubin platform: six new chips, all TSMC-made](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2026/01/07/2003850159)
**Source:** Taipei Times · **Date:** 2026-01-07

Why this matters: Six concurrent chips is a sharp departure from NVIDIA's old one-or-two-chip cadence and a clear signal of how much TSMC capacity it's locking up. Vera Rubin sets the architecture floor for the next two years of AI training and inference.

## 2. [NVIDIA set to overtake Apple as TSMC's largest customer](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/26/nvidia-set-to-supplant-apple-as-tsmcs-largest-customer.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-01-26

Why this matters: Apple has been TSMC's top customer for a decade. The handoff to NVIDIA is the single clearest data point that the semiconductor industry has reorganized around AI compute rather than consumer electronics.

## 3. [CES 2026: AMD, Qualcomm, NVIDIA debut new chips on TSMC 3nm and 5nm](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/01/05/news-amd-nvidia-and-others-reportedly-to-debut-new-chips-at-ces-2026-spotlighting-tsmc-3nm-and-5nm/)
**Source:** TrendForce · **Date:** 2026-01-05

Why this matters: The headline launches of CES were chip launches, not consumer gadgets. Intel's Panther Lake (18A process) is the company's most important test in years — if 18A holds, Intel re-enters the leading-edge conversation; if it doesn't, it's effectively done as a foundry competitor.

## 4. [TSMC Q4 earnings beat, capex guidance up, chip stocks rally](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/15/chip-stocks-nvidia-amd-pop-after-tsmcs-earnings-beat-lifts-confidence.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-01-15

Why this matters: 35% profit growth and raised 2026 capex put a number on the AI infrastructure buildout. When TSMC is willing to spend more, every downstream chip company gets more confident — and the supply-constrained AI buildout has more runway.

## 5. [Memory chip shortages drive a global semiconductor rally](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/06/semiconductor-stocks-rally-led-by-asml-tsmc-samsung.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-01-06

Why this matters: HBM and DRAM shortages are now constraining AI buildouts. ASML, TSMC, Samsung, and SK Hynix rallied together because the bottleneck has moved from logic chips to memory — and that shift reshapes who actually wins from the AI capex cycle.
