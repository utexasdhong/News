# Products & Agents — January 2026

## 1. [Anthropic launches Claude Cowork as a research preview](https://www.anthropic.com/)
**Source:** Anthropic · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Cowork is Claude Code with a GUI, aimed at non-technical users — the first real attempt to take "agent that runs on your computer" out of the developer audience and into general productivity workflows. Whether this lands or not, it's the template every other lab will copy or react to.

## 2. [Kore.ai launches Artemis AI agent platform](https://venturebeat.com/technology/kore-ai-launches-artemis-ai-agent-platform-expands-challenge-to-microsoft-and-salesforce)
**Source:** VentureBeat · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Kore.ai is going directly at Microsoft, Salesforce, and ServiceNow in the enterprise agent space, backed by a new strategic growth round led by AllianceBernstein. The enterprise agent market is starting to consolidate into a few platform plays — Artemis is one of them.

## 3. [ServiceNow + Accenture launch Forward Deployed Engineering for agentic AI](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2026/servicenow-and-accenture-launch-forward-deployed-engineering-program-to-scale-agentic-ai-across-the-enterprise)
**Source:** Accenture Newsroom · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: A model the AI labs popularized — embedding engineers directly with customers — is now being adopted by enterprise services giants. It signals agentic AI deployments are complex enough that catalog-style purchasing isn't working; customers need humans on-site to make it land.

## 4. [NVIDIA announces an open agent development platform at CES 2026](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/ai-agents)
**Source:** NVIDIA Newsroom · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: NVIDIA is positioning itself not just as the chip supplier but as the platform layer for building agents — competing directly with Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic for developer mindshare. When the picks-and-shovels vendor wants to be the platform, the market structure is unstable.
