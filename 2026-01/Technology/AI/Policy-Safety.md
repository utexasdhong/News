# Policy & Safety — January 2026

## 1. [EU member states issued €250M in AI Act fines in Q1 2026](https://www.metricstream.com/blog/ai-regulation-trends-ai-policies-us-uk-eu.html)
**Source:** MetricStream · **Date:** 2026-01 (Q1 figures)

Why this matters: 50 fines totaling €250 million in a single quarter — primarily targeting general-purpose AI non-compliance — shows the EU AI Act has teeth and is being enforced, not just announced. Vendor compliance work is now a real cost center, not a future risk item.

## 2. [EU AI Office: Llama Community License does not qualify as "free and open" under the AI Act](https://www.aipricingmaster.com/blog/Guide-to-Open-Source-AI-Models-in-2026)
**Source:** AI Pricing Master (citing EU AI Office advisory group) · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: The EU's legal interpretation strips Llama of "open source" status under the AI Act, which means Meta's models fall under the heavier GPAI obligations instead of the lighter open-source carveouts. Significant practical implications for any EU deployment built on Llama.

## 3. [Countdown to the EU AI Act's August 2 full application](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)
**Source:** European Commission · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Full application of the EU AI Act lands August 2, 2026 — January is when serious enterprise compliance programs hit crunch time. Watch GPAI provider obligations and the finalized Code of Practice; those will shape every model deployment in the EU.

## 4. [US AI regulation remains a state-by-state patchwork](https://www.gtlaw.com/en/insights/2025/12/2026-outlook-artificial-intelligence)
**Source:** Greenberg Traurig · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: With no federal AI law and EO 14179 setting only directional federal policy, more than a dozen states (California, Colorado, New York, Illinois, Utah, others) have enacted or proposed conflicting AI laws. Multistate compliance is now the biggest legal headache for any consumer-facing AI product in the US.
