# Climate — January 2026

## 1. [Trump administration moves to withdraw the US from the UN climate framework](https://unfccc.int/news/step-back-from-climate-cooperation-will-hurt-us-economy-statement-from-un-climate-chief-on-us)
**Source:** UNFCCC · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: A White House memorandum announced intent to withdraw the US from the UNFCCC along with 65 other international organizations. This goes further than first-term Paris withdrawal — it's a full pullback from the multilateral climate architecture. ([Carbon Brief context](https://www.carbonbrief.org/debriefed-9-january-2026-us-to-exit-global-climate-treaty-venezuelan-oil-uncertainty-hardest-truth-for-africas-energy-transition/))

## 2. [EU's carbon border levy (CBAM) takes full effect on January 1](https://www.carbonbrief.org/debriefed-9-january-2026-us-to-exit-global-climate-treaty-venezuelan-oil-uncertainty-hardest-truth-for-africas-energy-transition/)
**Source:** Carbon Brief · **Date:** 2026-01-01

Why this matters: The world's first major carbon border tariff is now in force, despite sharp opposition from trading partners and European industry. CBAM is a real-world test of whether carbon pricing can be projected beyond a jurisdiction's borders — every other major economy is watching.

## 3. [Deep federal climate-science cuts hit NOAA, NHC, NWS](https://blog.ucs.org/erika-spanger-siegfried/danger-season-is-here-again-with-triple-the-danger-for-2026/)
**Source:** Union of Concerned Scientists · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: Budget and staffing cuts to NOAA — including the National Hurricane Center and National Weather Service — are arriving just as the 2026 disaster season ramps up. The capability to forecast hurricanes and issue early warnings degrades in real time, with predictable consequences.

## 4. [Palisades and Eaton Fires confirmed as costliest wildfires on record globally](https://www.aon.com/en/insights/reports/climate-and-catastrophe-report)
**Source:** Aon Climate and Catastrophe Insight · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: $41 billion in insured losses from a single LA-area fire complex. The insurance industry's quantification of climate-driven loss is becoming the most credible economic signal in the policy debate — including for state insurance markets that may not survive another year like this.
