# Markets & Macro — January 2026

## 1. [Fed holds rates at 3.5–3.75%, pausing the 2025 cutting cycle](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/28/fed-rate-decision-january-2026.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-01-28

Why this matters: After three cuts in 2025, the Fed paused — citing sticky inflation balanced against a soaring unemployment rate. The committee is now genuinely caught between its two mandates, and that's why markets see low odds of a March cut even though futures price in two cuts over 2026. ([FOMC minutes](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260128.htm))

## 2. [S&P 500 ends January up 1.4%, hovering near 7,000](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/markets-and-economy/economy/fed-meeting-january-2026)
**Source:** J.P. Morgan · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: A positive January in a market dominated by AI capex and a paused Fed is a "wait and see" tape. Treasury yields ticked higher post-FOMC, signaling traders are taking the Fed's pause seriously and pricing in fewer cuts than they did at year-end 2025.

## 3. [Markets see two Fed cuts in 2026 — April and September](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/01/25/feds-expected-2026-rate-cuts-mean-stock-market/)
**Source:** Motley Fool (citing CME FedWatch) · **Date:** 2026-01-25

Why this matters: Futures-implied path is the single best leading indicator of what the rate-sensitive parts of the economy (housing, refinancing, regional banks, growth equities) actually price in. Two cuts vs. four is a meaningfully different 2026 for those sectors.

## 4. [Bulgaria joins the euro — eurozone now 21 members](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/January_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-01-01

Why this matters: A small economy in isolation, but the eurozone expanding (rather than fragmenting) during a period of CBAM rollouts and US tariff tensions sends a real signal about EU monetary cohesion. Bulgaria's adoption is the first new euro member in years.

## 5. [Safe-haven supercycle: gold and commodities at records as risk assets diverge](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/01/19/here-are-the-3-big-things-that-will-move-bitcoin-and-crypto-prices-in-2026)
**Source:** CoinDesk · **Date:** 2026-01-19

Why this matters: Gold and broader commodities pushed to record highs in January even as Bitcoin and equities wobbled. The cross-asset signal — flight to hard assets despite tame headline inflation — suggests investors are pricing in geopolitical and dollar-confidence risk that doesn't yet show up in CPI.
