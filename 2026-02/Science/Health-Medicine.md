# Health & Medicine — February 2026

## 1. [FDA grants accelerated approval to Yuviwel for achondroplasia](https://www.drugs.com/newdrugs.html)
**Source:** Drugs.com / FDA · **Date:** 2026-02-27

Why this matters: Navepegritide (Yuviwel), a C-type natriuretic peptide analog, is now an approved therapy for children with achondroplasia — the most common form of disproportionate short stature. Accelerated approval expands a treatment landscape that until recently had only Voxzogo, broadening options for a condition with no historical pharmacological standard of care.

## 2. [FDA issues draft framework for individualized therapies in ultra-rare disease](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-launches-framework-accelerating-development-individualized-therapies-ultra-rare-diseases)
**Source:** FDA · **Date:** 2026-02-23

Why this matters: Draft guidance covering n-of-1 genome editing, antisense oligonucleotides, and other patient-specific therapies. The framework attempts to reconcile traditional drug approval pathways with treatments designed for a single patient — a policy puzzle that has held back the most promising rare-disease research.

## 3. [FDA decision on first gene therapy for Hunter syndrome](https://www.thecardiologyadvisor.com/news/fda-drug-approval-decisions-expected-in-february-2026/)
**Source:** The Cardiology Advisor · **Date:** 2026-02-08

Why this matters: PDUFA target date for the first gene therapy for MPS II (Hunter syndrome), an X-linked lysosomal storage disorder. If approved, it joins a small group of approved gene therapies for inherited metabolic disease — and accelerates the curve for late-stage candidates targeting similar mechanisms.

## 4. [FDA fast-tracks multiple oncology candidates across bladder, breast, and other cancers](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/drugs/info/oncology-drugs-fast-tracked-by-the-fda-in-february-2026)
**Source:** Oncology News Central · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: A cluster of fast-track designations in February, particularly for immunotherapy-plus-DNA-polymerase-inhibitor combinations. The combo-therapy thesis — that mechanism stacking will outperform single-agent approvals — is increasingly visible in the FDA's expedited-pathway decisions.
