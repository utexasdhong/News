# Models — February 2026

## 1. [Google releases Gemini 3.1 Pro with 77% on ARC-AGI-2](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-1-pro/)
**Source:** Google Blog · **Date:** 2026-02-19

Why this matters: Gemini 3.1 Pro more than doubled its predecessor on ARC-AGI-2 (77.1% vs 31.1%) and topped GPQA Diamond at 94.3%. Pure-reasoning benchmarks have been the hardest thing to move with scale alone, so a single generation more than doubling them resets expectations about how close frontier models are to general problem-solving.

## 2. [Anthropic ships Claude Sonnet 4.6 at near-Opus quality](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-6)
**Source:** Anthropic · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Sonnet 4.6 leads the GDPval-AA Elo benchmark (1,633) at a mid-tier price point — a clear signal that "frontier" quality is migrating out of the flagship tier. For developers, it makes Opus-class agentic coding economically viable at production volume.

## 3. [xAI launches Grok 4.20 Beta with four-agent architecture](https://x.ai/news)
**Source:** xAI · **Date:** 2026-02-17

Why this matters: Grok 4.20 spins up four specialized agents (coordinator, researcher, logic, contrarian) in parallel and cross-verifies before answering. It's the most public commitment yet to multi-agent inference as the default response path — moving past "one model thinks really hard" toward built-in debate.
