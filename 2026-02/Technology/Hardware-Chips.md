# Hardware & Chips — February 2026

## 1. [Nvidia reports $68.1B Q4 revenue and shifts CPU strategy](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001045810/000104581026000019/q4fy26pr.htm)
**Source:** Nvidia (SEC 8-K) · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: Record Q4 (+73% YoY) and fiscal 2026 of $215.9B (+65% YoY). Equally important: Nvidia unveiled its standalone Vera CPU now deployed at Meta data centers — the first time the company has positioned a CPU as a serious revenue driver, signaling a pivot from "GPU vendor" to full data-center platform.

## 2. [AMD shares plunge 17% on weak forecast](https://mbrenndoerfer.com/writing/february-2026-selloff-anatomy-multi-trillion-dollar-wipeout)
**Source:** Brenndoerfer analysis · **Date:** 2026-02-04

Why this matters: AMD's miss helped trigger the broader software/AI selloff in February. The MI500 series narrative had set expectations very high; the gap between roadmap promises and near-term ramp is now a live concern for the AI-chip challenger story.

## 3. [Nvidia Grace Blackwell positioned as inference cost leader](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/nvidia-gtc-ai-jensen-huang-cpu-gpu.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: Huang told analysts Grace Blackwell delivers "order-of-magnitude lower cost per token" for inference. With "tokens generated" now an exponential curve, total-cost-of-inference has overtaken raw FLOPS as the metric customers and competitors track.

## 4. [Advantest discloses ransomware affecting semiconductor test ops](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/february-2026-recent-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks)
**Source:** Cyber Management Alliance · **Date:** 2026-02-15

Why this matters: Advantest is the dominant supplier of memory and SoC test equipment globally. A ransomware incident at a chokepoint in the semiconductor supply chain is a quiet but serious story — disruptions there ripple into every advanced-node fab's ramp schedule.
