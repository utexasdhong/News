# International Politics — May 2026

## 1. [Trump–Xi summit ends with Trump denying Xi warned Putin would regret invading Ukraine](https://www.russiamatters.org/news/russia-ukraine-war-report-card/russia-ukraine-war-report-card-may-20-2026)

## 2. [Russia loses net 69 sq mi of Ukrainian territory over four weeks](https://www.russiamatters.org/news/russia-ukraine-war-report-card/russia-ukraine-war-report-card-may-20-2026)
**Source:** Russia Matters · **Date:** 2026-05-20

Why this matters: A reversal of the 2024–2025 attritional dynamic. The Economist's tracker puts Ukrainian gains at 204 sq km over the past 30 days — modest, but it's the first sustained pushback in over a year, coinciding with European weapons packages backfilling the US aid pause.

## 3. [Lebanon holds its first parliamentary elections under President Aoun and PM Salam](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/1/1/all-the-big-elections-to-look-out-for-in-2026)
**Source:** Al Jazeera · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: The first major democratic test for the post-2025 government and a referendum on Hezbollah's role after the Israel-Hezbollah war and the broader Iran conflict. The result will shape the disarmament debate, the IMF program, and the post-war reconstruction settlement.

## 4. [UN General Assembly adopts climate resolution operationalizing the ICJ opinion](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/05/1167561)
**Source:** UN News · **Date:** 2026-05-20

Why this matters: Adopted 141–8 with 28 abstentions. The resolution endorses and seeks to operationalize the International Court of Justice's binding opinion on state climate obligations — a substantial diplomatic win for the Vanuatu-led coalition and a real headache for major emitters. Tone-sets the COP31 agenda.

## 5. [Colombia's presidential race shapes up: Cepeda vs. Fajardo vs. de la Espriella](https://www.cfr.org/articles/ten-elections-watch-2026)
**Source:** Council on Foreign Relations · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Petro's Historic Pact nominated Senator Iván Cepeda as its candidate, with center-left Fajardo and right-wing outsider de la Espriella as main competitors. The May ballot lineup decides whether Petro's left turn outlasts him, the trajectory of US–Colombia relations, and the future of the peace process with armed groups.
