## 1. [UN General Assembly operationalizes the ICJ climate opinion, 141–8](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/05/1167561)
**Source:** UN News · **Date:** 2026-05-20

Why this matters: The Vanuatu-led resolution endorses the ICJ's July 2025 binding opinion on state climate obligations and calls on every UN member to comply with international-law duties to avoid significant climate damage. ## 2. [April 2026 was the joint third-warmest April on record at 1.43°C above pre-industrial](https://earth.org/this-week-in-climate-news-may-2026-week-3/)
**Source:** Earth.Org · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Copernicus data published in May made it "virtually certain" that 2026 will end in the top 10 warmest years on record and likely in the top 5. The 1.5°C Paris guardrail keeps slipping — first crossed on an annual basis in 2024 — and 2026's trajectory says it isn't coming back below soon.

## 3. [South Africa hit by Orange-Level-8 floods; Kouga Dam exceeds 113% capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weather_of_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Weather of 2026 · **Date:** 2026-05-05

Why this matters: A cut-off low dropped over 300mm of rain in Joubertina between May 5–8, forcing mass evacuations in the Gamtoos Valley and causing widespread infrastructure damage across the Eastern Cape and Garden Route. Another data point in the intensifying pattern of single-event rainfall extremes in southern Africa.

## 4. [Trump council recommends overhauling FEMA — three changes for wildfires, floods, hurricanes](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/07/nx-s1-5803121/trump-fema-reform-wildfire-flood-hurricane)
**Source:** NPR · **Date:** 2026-05-07

Why this matters: A presidential council laid out the most significant proposed restructuring of FEMA in two decades — shifting disaster response cost to states, narrowing federal eligibility, and pushing risk pricing into building codes. With danger season upon us and 2026 fires already 20% above the prior January–April record, the timing of any structural cuts to FEMA is consequential.

## 5. [Munich Re: wildfire and storm losses keep climbing as insurers exit at-risk markets](https://www.munichre.com/en/company/media-relations/media-information-and-corporate-news/media-information/2026/natural-disaster-figures-2025.html)
**Source:** Munich Re · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: The world's largest reinsurer underlined that the loss curve is steepening, with intensifying wildfires and convective storms as the worst single drivers. The repricing of US wildfire risk has now begun to look like the Florida hurricane risk story did a decade ago.
