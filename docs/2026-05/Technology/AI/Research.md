# Research — May 2026

## 1. [Position paper argues agentic AI orchestration must be Bayes-consistent](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)
**Source:** arXiv · **Date:** 2026-05-04

Why this matters: Co-authored by 30 researchers from across industry, the paper argues that while making LLMs themselves Bayesian is computationally infeasible, the orchestration layer — the controller that decides which tools to invoke and how much compute to spend — needs principled probabilistic foundations. As agent systems balloon in deployed complexity, this is a load-bearing argument about where reliability has to come from.

## 2. [SciResearcher: scaling deep-research agents for frontier scientific reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01489)
**Source:** arXiv · **Date:** 2026-05-02

Why this matters: SciResearcher-8B sets a new state of the art at its parameter scale on HLE-Bio/Chem-Gold (19.46%) and posts 13–15% absolute gains on SuperGPQA-Hard-Biology and TRQA-Literature — beating several much larger proprietary agents. Concrete evidence that automated scientific-discovery agents are no longer just a demo class.

## 3. [JWST + Nature Astronomy: a massive early-universe galaxy with no rotation](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260506225135.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily / Nature Astronomy · **Date:** 2026-05-04

Why this matters: Published May 4 in Nature Astronomy, the study describes a galaxy formed less than 2 billion years after the Big Bang that shows essentially no spin — a property typically seen only in vastly older, more evolved galaxies. Forces a rethink of how angular momentum is acquired in early-universe galaxy formation.

## 4. [JWST maps the cosmic web of 164,000 galaxies back to the universe's first billion years](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260511213136.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily · **Date:** 2026-05-11

Why this matters: The COSMOS-Web survey produced the clearest map yet of the enormous filamentary structure connecting galaxies. The new map pushes detailed cosmic-web reconstruction back further in time than any prior dataset, sharpening tests of large-scale-structure predictions from cold-dark-matter cosmology.

## 5. [First detection of a repeating daily cloud cycle on a hot Jupiter](https://science.nasa.gov/mission/webb/latestnews/)
**Source:** Science / NASA · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: A May Science paper reported the first observed daily cloud cycle on the hot Jupiter WASP-94A b. Exoplanet atmospheres being resolved on a diurnal time scale moves the field from "we can detect atmospheres" to "we can do weather."
