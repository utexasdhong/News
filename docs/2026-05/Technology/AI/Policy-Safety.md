# Policy & Safety — May 2026

## 1. [EU Council and Parliament reach political agreement to streamline the AI Act](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/)
**Source:** Council of the EU · **Date:** 2026-05-07

Why this matters: The "Omnibus VII" deal pushes the high-risk AI rules to Dec 2, 2027 (standalone systems) and Aug 2, 2028 (embedded products), adds a new outright ban on non-consensual sexual/intimate AI content and CSAM, and clarifies the AI Office's supervisory remit. The biggest substantive rewrite of the EU AI Act since its passage — formal adoption expected before Aug 2, 2026.

## 2. [Anthropic publicly declines to release Mythos-class models, citing safety](https://www.theregister.com/security/2026/05/25/anthropic-to-release-mythos-class-models-to-the-public/5245596)
**Source:** The Register · **Date:** 2026-05-25

Why this matters: Anthropic stated that "no company — including Anthropic — has developed safeguards strong enough" to safely deploy Mythos, which can autonomously discover and exploit vulnerabilities within minutes. A frontier lab openly leaving capabilities on the shelf is rare and a real data point in the deploy-vs-pause debate. ([Just Security analysis](https://www.justsecurity.org/138011/too-dangerous-anthropic-mythos/))

## 3. [Trump administration moves toward AI oversight after Mythos disclosure](https://fortune.com/2026/05/06/trump-administration-embraces-ai-oversight-policies-it-once-rejected-anthropic-mythos-caisi/)
**Source:** Fortune · **Date:** 2026-05-06

Why this matters: National-security officials reportedly began drafting oversight policies the administration previously rejected, citing Mythos directly. It's the first concrete sign the Trump-era AI policy is bending toward intervention — driven not by Congress or activists but by frontier lab disclosures.

## 4. [Euronews explainer: what the EU's simplified AI Act actually changes](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/05/21/the-eu-simplified-its-toughest-ai-law-what-changed-and-why-it-matters)
**Source:** Euronews · **Date:** 2026-05-21

Why this matters: Useful plain-language guide to which provisions got softened, which got hardened (the CSAM/intimate-image bans), and which got delayed. For anyone shipping AI into Europe, this is the new compliance baseline through 2028.

## 5. [Project Glasswing expands to ~50 partners; reports 10,000+ critical bugs found](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing)
**Source:** Anthropic · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Anthropic's controlled-access program for Mythos has grown from 12 launch partners (Amazon, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Palo Alto Networks) to roughly 50 organizations. The first month produced over 10,000 high- or critical-severity vulnerability findings — proof that autonomous offensive AI capabilities already exist behind closed doors.
