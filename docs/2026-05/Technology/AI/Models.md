# Models — May 2026

## 1. [Google launches Gemini 3.5 Flash at I/O 2026, makes it default for 900M users](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-5/)
**Source:** Google Blog · **Date:** 2026-05-19

Why this matters: Gemini 3.5 Flash shipped GA the same day it was announced and immediately replaced gemini-3-flash-preview as the default model in the Gemini app and AI Mode in Google Search worldwide. It scored 76.2% on Terminal-Bench 2.1 and beats Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding/agent benchmarks at $1.50/$9 per 1M tokens — the clearest signal yet that "Flash-tier" is now where frontier intelligence lives for most users.

## 2. [Google unveils Gemini Omni — a natively multimodal model that generates from any input](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/google-io-2026-all-our-announcements/)
**Source:** Google · **Date:** 2026-05-19

Why this matters: Gemini Omni was framed as a leap in world understanding and editing — text in, video out; image in, audio out; any modality, any combination. Coupled with Gemini Spark (an always-on personal AI agent that runs in the background even on devices that are off), Google staked its claim that the next compute paradigm is ambient, multimodal, and agentic rather than chat-based.

## 3. [OpenAI ships GPT-5.5 Instant as the new default for all ChatGPT users](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/)
**Source:** OpenAI · **Date:** 2026-05-05

Why this matters: GPT-5.5 Instant rolled out to every ChatGPT tier — free included — replacing GPT-5.3 Instant. OpenAI reported 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts (medicine, law, finance) and 30% shorter outputs. Pushing a more accurate model to free users is one of the most consequential AI-product changes of the month by raw reach.

## 4. [Anthropic previews Claude Mythos — a model judged too dangerous to deploy](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/)
**Source:** Anthropic Red · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Mythos is an autonomous vulnerability-discovery model so capable that Anthropic explicitly says no company has safeguards strong enough to prevent misuse. Its first month inside Project Glasswing found 10,000+ high/critical-severity bugs across partner software. A frontier lab publicly choosing not to release a working model is itself a milestone for the safety-vs-deployment debate.

## 5. [Google releases Gemma 4 open-weights family for agentic workflows](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/google-io-2026-all-our-announcements/)
**Source:** Google · **Date:** 2026-05-04

Why this matters: Gemma 4 is engineered specifically for advanced reasoning and tool-use rather than general chat, and ships with permissive open weights. With Qwen and Llama-class models already saturating benchmarks, Google's push into open agentic foundations keeps pressure on the closed-model business case.
