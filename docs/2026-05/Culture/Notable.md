# Culture — May 2026

## 1. [Cristian Mungiu's "Fjord" wins the Palme d'Or at the 79th Cannes Film Festival](https://www.festival-cannes.com/en/press/press-releases/the-79th-festival-de-cannes-winners-list/)
**Source:** Festival de Cannes · **Date:** 2026-05-23

Why this matters: The Sebastian Stan / Renate Reinsve drama — about a Romanian immigrant family in Norway grappling with child abuse — gives Mungiu his second Palme, joining the small two-timer club. It's also NEON's seventh consecutive Palme d'Or distribution win, an unprecedented streak in the festival's history. ([Deadline coverage](https://deadline.com/2026/05/cannes-2026-winners-live-updating-1236919837/))

## 2. [Ted Turner, founder of CNN, dies at 87](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-05-06

Why this matters: Turner invented 24-hour news with CNN's 1980 launch, built the largest private landholding in the US, and reshaped American philanthropy with a $1B UN gift. He had been living with Lewy Body Dementia for eight years. The death of arguably the most consequential American media founder of the 20th century.

## 3. [Doris Fisher, co-founder of The Gap, dies at 94](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-05-02

Why this matters: Fisher named the company, ran merchandising for its first four decades, and was a defining figure of late-20th-century American casual retail. Her death closes a chapter on the era when family-built specialty retailers reshaped Main Street.

## 4. [Kyle Busch, two-time NASCAR Cup Series champion, dies at 41](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-05-21

Why this matters: Busch's death just hours after his family disclosed he was hospitalized with a "severe illness" stunned the motorsports world. A two-time Cup champion and one of NASCAR's most polarizing — and most popular — drivers of the past two decades.

## 5. [Alex Ligertwood, longtime Santana lead vocalist, dies at 79](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-05-01

Why this matters: Ligertwood fronted Santana from 1979 to 1994, the band's commercially defining era between the early classics and the Supernatural revival. His voice is on a generation's worth of stadium-rock staples.
