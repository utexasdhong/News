# Crypto & Fintech — May 2026

## 1. [Senate Banking Committee advances the CLARITY Act](https://news.bitcoin.com/this-week-in-crypto-law-may-16-2026/)
**Source:** Bitcoin.com News · **Date:** 2026-05-14

Why this matters: The most significant US digital-asset legislative step in years. CLARITY sorts every digital asset into one of three buckets — digital commodities (CFTC), investment-contract assets (SEC), or permitted payment stablecoins (banking regulators) — finally giving the industry a statutory taxonomy rather than enforcement-by-press-release. House action and a floor vote are the remaining hurdles.

## 2. [Bitcoin flash crash below $77K triggers $657M in liquidations](https://news.bitcoin.com/bitcoin-77000-657-million-liquidations-machi-big-brother-may-2026/)
**Source:** Bitcoin.com News · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: After breaking above $80,000 earlier in the month, BTC reversed sharply, with most damage in over-leveraged long positions. Spot ETFs registered a $1.039B net outflow for the May 11–15 week — the first net-outflow week after six consecutive weeks of inflows. A reminder that the institutionalization of crypto has not eliminated its mechanical volatility.

## 3. [JPMorgan to accept Bitcoin and Ether as collateral](https://www.oanda.com/us-en/trade-tap-blog/asset-classes/crypto/mid-month-crypto-update-may-2026/)
**Source:** OANDA · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Initially via ETF-based exposure, then expanding to spot holdings. The largest US bank treating crypto as collateral-eligible inside its prime brokerage is one of the biggest TradFi-DeFi bridge moves of the cycle — and a quiet pricing-power victory for the spot Bitcoin ETF complex.

## 4. [SoFi becomes the first US chartered bank to offer direct digital-asset trading from customer accounts](https://www.oanda.com/us-en/trade-tap-blog/asset-classes/crypto/mid-month-crypto-update-may-2026/)
**Source:** OANDA · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Chartered bank, FDIC umbrella, direct crypto trading from a checking account — a regulatory configuration that didn't exist 12 months ago. Tests whether community/regional banks can follow without separate trust company structures.

## 5. [SEC rescinds Rule 202.5(e) — defendants no longer gagged from denying allegations](https://news.bitcoin.com/this-week-in-crypto-law-may-16-2026/)
**Source:** Bitcoin.com News · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Ends a 50-year-old practice requiring settling defendants to refrain from publicly denying SEC allegations. Cuts to the heart of how the SEC has handled crypto cases for the past five years — settling parties can now contest the agency's narrative publicly without breaching the deal.
