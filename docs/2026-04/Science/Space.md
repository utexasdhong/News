# Space — April 2026

## 1. [Artemis II becomes the farthest human spaceflight in history](https://www.nasa.gov/2026-news-releases/)
**Source:** NASA · **Date:** 2026-04-06

Why this matters: On April 6, Artemis II surpassed the distance record set by Apollo 13 in 1970, becoming the farthest humans have traveled from Earth. The crewed lunar flyby splashed down successfully aboard the Integrity capsule on April 11. After years of delays and cost overruns, NASA returned humans to deep space — a prerequisite for Artemis III's planned 2027 lunar landing.

## 2. [SpaceX Falcon Heavy returns to flight, delivering ViaSat-3 F3 to GEO](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/launches-spacecraft/spacex-falcon-heavy-rocket-viasat-3-f3-launch)
**Source:** Space.com · **Date:** 2026-04-29

Why this matters: First Falcon Heavy launch in 18 months, delivering a high-throughput communications satellite into geostationary orbit. Falcon Heavy remains the workhorse for the heaviest GEO payloads while Starship continues its iterative test campaign — and the long gap between Falcon Heavy flights reflects how thoroughly Starship has dominated SpaceX's R&D bandwidth.

## 3. [SpaceX launches Cygnus XL on NG-24 cargo mission to the ISS](https://www.nasa.gov/2026-news-releases/)
**Source:** NASA · **Date:** 2026-04-09

Why this matters: A Falcon 9 lofted Northrop Grumman's new Cygnus XL — the larger-volume variant — with 8,200 lbs of supplies and experiments to the ISS. With the ISS scheduled for deorbit in 2030, every cargo mission now also serves as a final-years science campaign and a test bed for commercial-station logistics.

## 4. [Operational tempo: ~10 SpaceX launches in April, ongoing Starlink buildout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_in_spaceflight)
**Source:** Wikipedia (2026 in spaceflight) · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: SpaceX maintained roughly two launches per week in April, mostly Starlink deployments. The cadence is the story — orbital launch has become a routine industrial activity rather than a spectacle, with Starlink now approaching ~8,000 active satellites and direct-to-cell service rolling out market by market.

## 5. [Artemis II crew completes post-flight medical and engineering debriefs feeding into Artemis III planning](https://www.nasa.gov/2026-news-releases/)
**Source:** NASA · **Date:** 2026-04 (post-flight)

Why this matters: The April debriefs identify which Orion subsystems performed as expected (life support, thermal, comms during the trans-lunar trajectory) and which need design changes before the 2027 landing attempt. Each item from the Artemis II punch list either confirms or slips the Artemis III schedule.
