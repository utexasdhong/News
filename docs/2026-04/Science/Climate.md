# Climate — April 2026

## 1. [April 2026 ranks as the fourth-warmest April on record globally](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-briefings/april-2026)
**Source:** Climate Central · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: Climate Central's monthly briefing confirms April 2026 as the 4th-warmest April in the global record, with all 50 US states and 99% of 242 analyzed cities having warmed since the first Earth Day in 1970. Human-caused warming is the documented driver in 98% of US cities — a record that increasingly forces local policy responses regardless of federal direction.

## 2. [Renewables overtook coal in global electricity generation for 2025, per Ember's Global Electricity Review](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-briefings/april-2026)
**Source:** Ember Global Electricity Review 2026 (via Climate Central) · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: Ember's April report confirms that in 2025, clean-power growth exceeded the rise in overall global electricity demand for the first time, with fossil generation declining and renewables surpassing coal. A genuine inflection point in the energy transition — the first year decarbonization moved faster than load growth.

## 3. [Trump fires all 22 members of the National Science Board](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_in_climate_change)
**Source:** Wikipedia (2026 in climate change) · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: The NSB governs the NSF's grant priorities and advises Congress on US scientific direction. Universities and national labs are now drafting budget contingencies for an uncertain NSF.

## 4. [Colombia and the Netherlands co-host the first International Conference on the Just Transition Away from Fossil Fuels](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-briefings/april-2026)
**Source:** Climate Central · **Date:** 2026-04-28 to 2026-04-29

Why this matters: Held in Santa Marta, Colombia — a working oil port — the first ministerial-level "just transition away from fossil fuels" conference produced concrete commitments on financing transition costs for resource-dependent economies. Colombia is positioning itself as the Global South leader on transition policy ahead of COP31.

## 5. [Study: RCP8.5 worst-case emissions scenario now "implausible"](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-briefings/april-2026)
**Source:** Geoscientific Model Development (via Climate Central) · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: A peer-reviewed study concludes that the IPCC's most extreme baseline scenario — RCP8.5, widely used in climate-risk literature — is no longer plausible given renewable cost declines and policy trajectories. Reframes climate-impact projections downward at the worst-case tail while keeping mid-range warming concerns intact. Re-anchors a decade of risk modeling.
