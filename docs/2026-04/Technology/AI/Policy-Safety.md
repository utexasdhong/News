# Policy & Safety — April 2026

## 1. [EU AI Act trilogue ends without agreement on the August 2026 deadline](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/us-companies-face-eu-ai-acts-possible-august-2026-compliance-deadline)
**Source:** Holland & Knight · **Date:** 2026-04-28

Why this matters: The second political trilogue between the European Parliament, Council, and Commission on April 28 ended without agreement on whether to delay the AI Act's August 2 applicability date. If no deal is struck before August, the original deadline stands — and US firms deploying general-purpose AI in the EU are suddenly on a four-month compliance sprint covering transparency, model documentation, and high-risk obligations.

## 2. [Anthropic withholds Claude Mythos over cyber-risk concerns](https://renovateqr.com/blog/ai-models-april-2026)
**Source:** Renovate QR release roundup · **Date:** 2026-04-07

Why this matters: Mythos identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities autonomously during evaluation. Anthropic chose not to ship publicly — only restricted access via "Project Glasswing." First time a major lab has held back a frontier model on real, articulated safety grounds rather than capabilities theatre. Sets a precedent regulators will reference.

## 3. [California EO N-5-26 directs state agencies to draft AI safety procurement rules](https://lumichats.com/blog/ai-regulation-usa-2026-executive-orders-laws-explained)
**Source:** LumiChats / California EO summary · **Date:** 2026-03-30 (effects unfold in April)

Why this matters: Newsom's EO directs every state agency to draft AI safety requirements — covering illegal content, bias, civil rights — that companies must meet to do business with California. With no federal AI law and Texas/Florida moving in opposite directions, California's procurement leverage is again becoming de facto national regulation.

## 4. [The White House's National AI Policy Framework continues rolling out through April](https://lumichats.com/blog/ai-regulation-usa-2026-executive-orders-laws-explained)
**Source:** LumiChats US AI regulation summary · **Date:** 2026-04 (framework released 2026-03-20)

Why this matters: The framework released March 20 contains sweeping legislative recommendations for a "nationally unified" approach to AI governance — and federal agencies spent April beginning implementation guidance. It's the Trump administration's substitute for the rescinded Biden EO 14110: lighter-touch, innovation-first, pre-empting state rules where possible.

## 5. [AI Compliance Risk in 2026: 10 critical areas for enterprises](https://www.kiteworks.com/cybersecurity-risk-management/ai-regulation-2026-business-compliance-guide/)
**Source:** Kiteworks · **Date:** 2026-04

Why this matters: A useful synthesis of where compliance pain actually lives now: model documentation, training-data provenance, copyright, biometric use, agent-action liability, cross-border data, watermarking. Useful as a roadmap for what frontier-lab and enterprise-buyer legal teams are actually arguing about this quarter.
