## 2. [Iran's deadliest crackdown since 1979 amid nationwide protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/January_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events (multi-source) · **Date:** 2026-01

Why this matters: An economic-crisis-driven protest wave was met with the most violent state response since the Islamic Republic was founded, with death tolls estimated between 12,000 and 30,000. The legitimacy crisis is acute, and any successor scenario reshapes the entire Middle East.

## 3. [Uganda's election: Museveni wins reelection amid fraud allegations](https://www.controlrisks.com/our-thinking/geopolitical-calendar)
**Source:** Control Risks · **Date:** 2026-01-15

Why this matters: Museveni has held office since 1986 — his reelection (with credible fraud allegations and protests) extends one of Africa's longest-running personalist regimes and tees up another contested transition whenever it eventually comes.

## 4. [Bulgaria adopts the euro, becoming the 21st eurozone member](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/January_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-01-01

Why this matters: First eurozone expansion in years. Symbolically meaningful for EU integration during a period of EU strain (CBAM disputes, AI Act enforcement, US tariff threats) — a reminder that EU institutions are still pulling members deeper rather than fragmenting.
