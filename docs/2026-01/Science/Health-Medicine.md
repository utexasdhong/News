# Health & Medicine — January 2026

## 1. [FDA grants breakthrough therapy designation to Alkermes' alixorexton for narcolepsy](https://www.neurologylive.com/view/fda-action-update-january-2026-approvals-acceptance-and-clearance)
**Source:** NeurologyLive · **Date:** 2026-01-06

Why this matters: Alixorexton is an oral, selective orexin-2 receptor agonist — a mechanism that directly targets the underlying biology of Type 1 narcolepsy rather than just managing symptoms. Breakthrough designation accelerates review for what could be the first true disease-modifying narcolepsy treatment.

## 2. [Bayer's sevabertinib (Hyrnuo) gets breakthrough designation for HER2-mutant NSCLC](https://www.neurologylive.com/view/fda-action-update-january-2026-approvals-acceptance-and-clearance)
**Source:** NeurologyLive (FDA action update) · **Date:** 2026-01-06

Why this matters: HER2-mutant non-small cell lung cancer has historically had limited targeted options. Sevabertinib joins a small but growing list of HER2-directed therapies for lung cancer — a step toward making lung cancer a tractable, mutation-targeted disease the way breast cancer has become.

## 3. [Bezuclastinib + sunitinib breakthrough designation for GIST](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/drugs/info/key-oncology-drugs-granted-fda-designations-in-january-2026)
**Source:** Oncology News Central · **Date:** 2026-01-26

Why this matters: The phase 3 PEAK trial showed a 50% reduction in risk of progression or death versus sunitinib alone in patients with prior-imatinib-treated gastrointestinal stromal tumors. That's a substantial efficacy gain in a previously stalled treatment area.

## 4. [FDA PDUFA date for tabelecleucel — first allogeneic EBV-specific T-cell therapy](https://www.neurologylive.com/view/fda-action-update-january-2026-approvals-acceptance-and-clearance)
**Source:** NeurologyLive (FDA action update) · **Date:** 2026-01-10

Why this matters: An off-the-shelf, donor-derived T-cell therapy for EBV-positive post-transplant lymphoproliferative disease. Off-the-shelf cell therapies (vs. patient-specific CAR-T) could finally make cell therapy affordable and scalable — this is one of the first approvals to watch.
