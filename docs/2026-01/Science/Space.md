# Space — January 2026

## 1. [NASA launches the Pandora space telescope to study exoplanet atmospheres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_spaceflight_launches_in_January%E2%80%93March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia (spaceflight launches Q1 2026) · **Date:** 2026-01-11

Why this matters: Pandora is a dedicated small-satellite mission focused on understanding stellar contamination in exoplanet atmosphere measurements — a key bottleneck for JWST-era biosignature science. It rode on a Falcon 9 with SPARCS and BlackCAT, a CubeSat-heavy science cadence becoming the new normal.

## 2. [SpaceX kicks off 2026 with Cosmo-SkyMed launch for Italy](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/01/02/live-coverage-spacex-opens-2026-with-launch-of-cosmo-skymed-earth-observation-satellite-for-italy/)
**Source:** Spaceflight Now · **Date:** 2026-01-02

Why this matters: Dual-use (civilian + military) Earth observation for a NATO ally on a US commercial rocket. The Cosmo-SkyMed Second Generation Flight Model 3 is the third of four; the constellation is part of how Europe sees imagery sovereignty in a contested geopolitical environment.

## 3. [Orbital Paradigm flies its first space-capsule technology demo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_spaceflight_launches_in_January%E2%80%93March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia (spaceflight launches Q1 2026) · **Date:** 2026-01-12

Why this matters: A Spanish space-capsule company, backed by ESA's Future Launchers Preparatory Programme, flying its first demonstration. Europe is finally building independent crew-and-return capability — a long-running strategic gap relative to the US, China, and India.
