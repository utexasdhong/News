# Notable Culture — February 2026

## 1. [Robert Duvall, Oscar-winning actor, dies at 95](https://www.livenowfox.com/news/celebrity-deaths-2026-list)
**Source:** LiveNOW from FOX · **Date:** 2026-02-15

Why this matters: Duvall's six-decade career — "The Godfather," "Apocalypse Now," "Tender Mercies" (Best Actor Oscar) — bracketed the second half of American cinema's century. His death marks the closing of the Coppola-era ensemble bench, and reignited retrospectives across major outlets.

## 2. [Neil Sedaka dies at 86](https://www.livenowfox.com/news/celebrity-deaths-2026-list)
**Source:** LiveNOW from FOX · **Date:** 2026-02-27

Why this matters: Brill Building songwriter and singer behind "Breaking Up Is Hard to Do," "Laughter in the Rain," and a catalog that helped define early-1960s American pop. One of the last major figures from that songwriting era to pass.

## 3. ["Yellow Letters" by Ilker Çatak wins Golden Bear at Berlinale 2026](https://variety.com/2026/film/news/berlin-film-festival-awards-ceremony-updating-live-1236669134/)
**Source:** Variety · **Date:** 2026-02-21

Why this matters: First Golden Bear for a German film in 22 years. The political drama follows a left-leaning Turkish couple drawing state attention in Ankara — a pointed selection given Berlin's reputation for politically engaged programming. Sandra Hüller won Best Performance for "Rose."

## 4. [68th Grammy Awards held](https://totalmusicawards.com/uncategorized/list-of-music-award-shows/)
**Source:** Total Music Awards · **Date:** 2026-02-01

Why this matters: The first major awards show of the calendar year sets the tone for the rest of awards season and historically previews tour and streaming-momentum stories for the year. February 1's ceremony anchored the music-industry calendar around the year-opening narrative.

## 5. [Willie Colón, Nuyorican salsa pioneer, dies at 75](https://www.livenowfox.com/news/celebrity-deaths-2026-list)
**Source:** LiveNOW from FOX · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Colón's trombone, his decades-long collaboration with Héctor Lavoe, and the Fania Records canon defined New York salsa. His death prompted tributes across the Latin music world and renewed interest in the social-protest tradition of 1970s salsa.
