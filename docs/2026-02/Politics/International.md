# International Politics — February 2026

## 1. [BNP wins Bangladesh general election in landslide](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/13/bangladesh-election-results-2026-who-won-who-lost-whats-next)
**Source:** Al Jazeera · **Date:** 2026-02-12

Why this matters: First election since Sheikh Hasina was ousted by student-led protests in August 2024. BNP, led by Tarique Rahman, took 208 seats — a two-thirds supermajority. Jamaat-e-Islami's 69 seats marked its best showing in history. Hasina's Awami League was barred. A constitutional referendum ran in parallel. South Asia's third-largest democracy just realigned.

## 2. [Rob Jetten sworn in as Dutch prime minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/February_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-02-23

Why this matters: At 38, Jetten became the youngest PM in Dutch history — and the first openly gay one. Comes after extended coalition talks following the November 2025 election. Symbolically and substantively a generational handover, and a check on the rightward trend in nearby European politics.

## 3. [New START Treaty between US and Russia expires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/February_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-02-05

Why this matters: For the first time since 1972, no bilateral US-Russia treaty caps strategic nuclear arsenals. Combined with collapsed INF/Open Skies, the global arms-control architecture is effectively gone. The downstream effect on stockpile decisions and modernization budgets is the strategic story of the decade.

## 4. [Trump pushes Zelenskyy to accept Donbas concessions for quick deal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_United_States%E2%80%93Ukraine%E2%80%93Russia_meetings_in_Geneva)
**Source:** Wikipedia · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: Trump told Zelenskyy by phone he wanted the war ended within a month, after Zelenskyy disclosed both US and Russian sides were pressuring Ukraine to cede the rest of Donbas. Talks rotated through the UAE and Switzerland but produced no breakthrough on territory. The pressure-campaign mode is now the defining pattern of US Ukraine diplomacy.

## 5. [VP JD Vance becomes highest-ranking US official ever to visit Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/February_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Vance met PM Pashinyan on the Nagorno-Karabakh peace deal implementation. The visit signals US interest in supplanting Russian influence in the South Caucasus and is a notable preview of how Vance is being used internationally.
