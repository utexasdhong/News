# Climate — February 2026

## 1. [Nature Geoscience: ENSO transition explained 75% of 2022–2023 energy uptake spike](https://www.nature.com/nclimate/articles?year=2026)
**Source:** Nature Geoscience · **Date:** 2026-02-12

Why this matters: A major attribution study quantifying how much of the record-breaking 2023 heat was the La Niña–to–El Niño swing versus underlying warming. Sharper attribution work directly affects how policymakers and insurers price climate risk going forward — and how much of "this year was hot" can be tied to anthropogenic forcing vs. natural variability.

## 2. [Nature Ecology & Evolution: long-term warming cut Northern Hemisphere fish biomass up to 19.8%](https://www.nature.com/nclimate/articles?year=2026)
**Source:** Nature Ecology & Evolution · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: Annual fish biomass in major Northern Hemisphere basins declined up to 19.8% between 1993 and 2021. This is one of the largest empirical estimates of warming-driven fisheries impact yet — relevant for food security, FAO catch-quota policy, and the economics of coastal fishing communities globally.

## 3. [Storm Nils causes severe flooding across western France](https://www.greenpeace.org/international/story/82686/climate-emergencies-photos-of-2026-so-far/)
**Source:** Greenpeace International · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: The entire western half of France fell under severe rain and flood warnings as Storm Nils struck. Combined with the Peruvian floods (~5,500 homes affected) and Chile's record wildfire season, February underlined that 2026 is opening as another high-cost compound-disaster year for global insurers and emergency-response systems.

## 4. [WMO: snow drought and Utah heat wave drop early-February snowpack to one-third of normal](https://wmo.int/media/news/extreme-heat-cold-precipitation-and-fires-mark-start-of-2026)
**Source:** WMO · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Utah's anomalously low early-February snowpack — about a third of the typical level, with rapid melt — is one of the more concrete signals of the western US water-supply outlook for summer 2026. Snowpack is the dominant input to the Colorado River system that supplies 40M people downstream.

## 5. [PLOS One: tropical flowering shifting 2 days earlier per decade](https://www.nature.com/nclimate/articles?year=2026)
**Source:** PLOS One · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: A 230-year dataset (1794–2024) shows tropical plants are advancing flowering at a rate comparable to temperate, boreal, alpine, and desert biomes. Previously, tropical ecosystems were thought to be more buffered against phenological shifts. The new data closes a major gap in climate-ecology models.
