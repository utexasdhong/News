# Markets & Macro — February 2026

## 1. [Supreme Court strikes down IEEPA tariffs in 6-3 ruling](https://www.scotusblog.com/2026/02/supreme-court-strikes-down-tariffs/)
**Source:** SCOTUSblog · **Date:** 2026-02-20

Why this matters: The Court invalidated the legal basis for over $200B in tariffs collected since 2025. Trump immediately invoked Section 122 to impose a 15% global tariff — but the refund question, plus the precedent against expansive IEEPA economic authority, is the single biggest macro/legal event of February. CFR called it "the Supreme Court clipping Trump's tariff powers."

## 2. [S&P 500 turns negative for 2026 as software stocks lose ~$1T](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/stock-market-today-live-updates.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-02-04

Why this matters: S&P 500 lost 1.23% (closing at 6,798) to flip negative YTD; Nasdaq slid 1.59%. The iShares software ETF was down 28% from its high. The trigger: hyperscaler 2026 capex disclosures ($175–185B Alphabet, $200B Amazon) hitting at the same time as an AMD warning. The "AI lifts everything" narrative cracked in real time.

## 3. [Nvidia reports record $68.1B Q4, $215.9B fiscal year (+65% YoY)](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001045810/000104581026000019/q4fy26pr.htm)
**Source:** Nvidia (SEC 8-K) · **Date:** 2026-02-25

Why this matters: Counter-narrative to the broader selloff. Nvidia's revenue still growing >70% YoY at this scale is the data point bulls cling to; the gap between Nvidia's results and the rest of the software complex is now the defining anomaly of public-market AI exposure.

## 4. [Fed holds, FOMC marks up 2026 growth and inflation forecasts](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260128.htm)
**Source:** Federal Reserve · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: At the late-January FOMC carried into February's commentary, the Fed held IOER at 3.65% and projected inflation ticking up 0.1pp to 2.7% for 2026 with stronger growth. The "higher for longer" backdrop is now baseline for risk-asset pricing through 2026.

## 5. [February sets record $189B in global VC funding, 83% AI-driven](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/record-setting-global-funding-february-2026-openai-anthropic/)
**Source:** Crunchbase News · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Anthropic's $30B and Waymo's $16B alone accounted for the bulk of activity, with public software simultaneously selling off. The widening divergence between private AI capital deployment and public market sentiment is the defining structural story of the cycle.
