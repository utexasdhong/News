# AI Policy & Safety — February 2026

## 1. [EU Commission floats one-year delay of AI Act high-risk provisions](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/us-companies-face-eu-ai-acts-possible-august-2026-compliance-deadline)
**Source:** Holland & Knight Insights · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: The August 2026 high-risk AI compliance deadline is the most consequential single regulatory event of the year for global AI vendors. The Commission's signal that it may push high-risk rules to August 2027 — under pressure from US trade tensions and member-state industry lobbying — is a major shift in the regulatory calendar.

## 2. [EU member states issue €250M in Q1 2026 GPAI fines](https://www.metricstream.com/blog/ai-regulation-trends-ai-policies-us-uk-eu.html)
**Source:** MetricStream Trends Report · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: 50 fines totaling a quarter-billion euros in Q1 alone, with Ireland (home to most major EU tech HQs) handling 60% of cases. The AI Act is no longer hypothetical compliance work — it's an active enforcement regime, and the burden is concentrating on a handful of jurisdictions.

## 3. [Microsoft patches Copilot DLP bypass that exposed confidential emails to AI](https://securityboulevard.com/2026/02/microsoft-patches-security-flaw-that-exposed-confidential-emails-to-ai/)
**Source:** Security Boulevard · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: Since January, Copilot had been able to read and summarize emails labeled confidential, bypassing Microsoft 365 Data Loss Prevention controls. Cyber Pulse data showed 80% of Fortune 500 deploying AI agents, but only 47% with adequate generative-AI security controls. AI-agent data exfiltration is the new insider-threat surface.

## 4. [White House signals national AI policy framework forthcoming](https://www.gtlaw.com/en/insights/2025/12/2026-outlook-artificial-intelligence)
**Source:** Greenberg Traurig 2026 Outlook · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: With no comprehensive federal AI law and California/New York enacting their own frontier-model rules, the White House's pending National Policy Framework (released March) was foreshadowed throughout February in congressional hearings. The federal-vs-state preemption fight is now the central US AI-governance battle.
