# AI Research — February 2026

> Slim month — fewer prominent peer-reviewed breakthroughs than a typical month; most signal in February came from model and product releases rather than papers.

## 1. [Physics-informed ML algorithm improves fluid dynamics and climate modeling](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/ai-breakthroughs-and-latest-ai-news-2026)
**Source:** AIP Advances via Crescendo AI · **Date:** 2026-02

Why this matters: A University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa team published a method that constrains ML models to obey physical laws even when training data is sparse. Unlike black-box models, outputs stay physically plausible — directly useful for engineering, meteorology, and renewable-energy planning where extrapolation matters more than interpolation.

## 2. [La Niña-to-El Niño transition explained 75% of Earth's 2022–2023 energy uptake spike](https://www.nature.com/nclimate/articles?year=2026)
**Source:** Nature Geoscience · **Date:** 2026-02-12

Why this matters: Using climate-data-trained ML attribution methods, researchers untangled how much of the record 2023 heat was the ENSO swing vs. underlying warming. This kind of attribution work is what climate-policy debates increasingly hinge on, and it's getting sharper.

## 3. [Gemini 3.1 Pro doubles SOTA on ARC-AGI-2](https://deepmind.google/models/model-cards/gemini-3-1-pro/)
**Source:** Google DeepMind · **Date:** 2026-02-19

Why this matters: ARC-AGI-2 is designed to be hard for memorization and easy for reasoning. A single generation moving from 31% to 77% is the kind of jump researchers had been arguing would require new architectures — that it came from scaling and tooling improvements within the Gemini family will reshape research priorities.
