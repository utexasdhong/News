# Major World Events — March 2026

## 1. [Ali Khamenei killed in joint US-Israeli strike; Mojtaba Khamenei installed as Supreme Leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: The death of Iran's Supreme Leader and the bombing campaign that followed — striking 153 cities and 500+ sites and killing 787 by the end of March — is the largest single geopolitical event since January's Venezuela operation. Iran's installation of Khamenei's son resolved succession but locked in a war footing. Forty days of national mourning declared.

## 2. [Iran IRGC declares the Strait of Hormuz "closed"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: An IRGC commander said any ships attempting to cross the channel would be set on fire. About 20% of global oil flows through Hormuz; even partial disruption is the most acute commodity-supply shock since 1973. Global energy prices climbed; inflation forecasts moved; central banks recalibrated.

## 3. [Israel launches ground offensive into Lebanon after Hezbollah attacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Hezbollah's drone and rocket attacks triggered an IDF ground offensive into southern Lebanon, capturing positions along the border. Israeli strikes displaced 370,000+ children, with 120+ children killed and nearly 400 injured in March alone. The northern front of the regional war became fully active.

## 4. [India wins back-to-back Men's T20 World Cup over New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: India beat New Zealand by 96 runs to become the first team ever to win consecutive T20 World Cups. Beyond the sport, the result lands at a moment when cricket's center of gravity (and its commercial power) is now decisively in Indian hands.

## 5. [Trump-Xi bilateral meeting headlines March diplomatic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Current_events/March_2026)
**Source:** Wikipedia Current Events · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: A direct Trump-Xi meeting — set against the IEEPA tariff ruling, the Section 122 global tariff, and the active Iran war — is the highest-stakes US-China engagement of the year so far. Outcomes (or lack thereof) will shape trade and Taiwan posture for the remainder of 2026.
