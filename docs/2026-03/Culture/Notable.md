# Culture — March 2026

## 1. ["One Battle After Another" wins Best Picture at the 98th Academy Awards](https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2026)
**Source:** Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences · **Date:** 2026-03-15

Why this matters: Paul Thomas Anderson's "One Battle After Another" swept the night with six wins including Best Picture, Directing, and Adapted Screenplay. Michael B. Jordan won Best Actor for "Sinners"; Jessie Buckley won Best Actress for "Hamnet"; Sean Penn won Supporting Actor; Amy Madigan won Supporting Actress for "Weapons." Conan O'Brien hosted for the second year in a row.

## 2. [Academy introduces Best Casting Oscar — first new competitive category since 2001](https://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2026)
**Source:** Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences · **Date:** 2026-03-15

Why this matters: The first new competitive Oscar category since Best Animated Feature in 2001 honors casting directors, formally recognizing a craft that the Academy has long ignored. A small but structural change in how the film industry credits creative labor.

## 3. [James Tolkan, "Back to the Future" and "Top Gun" actor, dies at 94](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-03-26

Why this matters: Tolkan's Mr. Strickland and Stinger were defining 1980s screen presences — terse, authoritative, instantly recognizable. His death closes the door on a particular character-actor archetype that shaped two of the most-watched American films of the era.

## 4. [Songwriter Chip Taylor and actress Valerie Perrine die on the same day](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-03-23

Why this matters: Chip Taylor wrote "Wild Thing" and "Angel of the Morning" — songs that crossed garage rock and country into the standard pop canon. Valerie Perrine earned an Oscar nomination for "Lenny" and played Miss Teschmacher in the original "Superman." Two pillars of 1960s-70s American pop culture gone in 24 hours.

## 5. [Motörhead guitarist Phil Campbell dies at 64](https://www.legacy.com/groups/notable-deaths/celebrities)
**Source:** Legacy.com · **Date:** 2026-03-13

Why this matters: Campbell played guitar with Motörhead from 1984 until Lemmy's death in 2015 — over three decades as the sonic backbone of one of the most uncompromising bands in rock. His death closes a near-complete chapter on Motörhead's classic-era lineup.
