# Cybersecurity — March 2026

## 1. [TELUS Digital hit by ShinyHunterz — 1 petabyte of customer data exfiltrated](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/biggest-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-of-march-2026)
**Source:** Cyber Management Alliance · **Date:** 2026-03-11

Why this matters: A single attack pulled roughly 1 PB — including source code, FBI background checks, financials, voice recordings, and Salesforce data belonging to TELUS Digital's BPO customers. The volume itself is a watershed, and the FBI-background-check exposure makes this a national-security incident, not just a corporate breach.

## 2. [Iranian-linked Handala wipes 200,000+ Stryker devices across 79 countries](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/biggest-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-of-march-2026)
**Source:** Cyber Management Alliance · **Date:** 2026-03-12

Why this matters: Handala, an Iranian-linked group, triggered simultaneous factor resets on 200,000+ Stryker corporate devices, disrupting a major medical-device manufacturer at global scale. Against the backdrop of the active US-Israel-Iran conflict, this is cyber-as-warfare hitting healthcare supply chains — a different category from typical ransomware.

## 3. [March 2026 sets ransomware record with 808 victims across 65 groups](https://www.breachsense.com/ransomware-reports/march-2026/)
**Source:** Breachsense · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Breachsense logged 808 ransomware victims (+19% MoM) with Qilin alone hitting 131, Akira 84, TheGentlemen 64. The persistent month-over-month growth — and the rise of new groups — signals that takedowns of LockBit, ALPHV, and others have produced ecosystem fragmentation, not deterrence.

## 4. [Qilin ransomware breaches German political party Die Linke](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/biggest-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-of-march-2026)
**Source:** Cyber Management Alliance · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: Russian-speaking Qilin hit Die Linke, forcing IT shutdowns to limit spread. Political-party targeting by Russian-aligned ransomware crews — outside an election window — is a continued signal that the line between criminal and state-aligned cyber actors keeps blurring.

## 5. [Foster City, CA paused public services after ransomware attack](https://www.cm-alliance.com/cybersecurity-blog/biggest-cyber-attacks-data-breaches-ransomware-attacks-of-march-2026)
**Source:** Cyber Management Alliance · **Date:** 2026-03-19

Why this matters: A US municipality of ~33,000 had to suspend non-emergency public services after a ransomware hit. Small and mid-size cities remain the soft underbelly of the public sector — and the cumulative effect of these incidents is what drives federal CISA budget fights.
