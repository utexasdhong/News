# Climate — March 2026

## 1. [Record-shattering March heat in Western North America "virtually impossible without climate change"](https://www.worldweatherattribution.org/record-shattering-march-temperatures-in-western-north-america-virtually-impossible-without-climate-change/)
**Source:** World Weather Attribution · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: A heat dome pushed temperatures 11-17°C above average across California, Nevada, and Arizona; Phoenix saw multiple consecutive ~41°C days, far above the prior all-time March record. NOAA's NCEI ranked it the warmest March in the contiguous US 132-year record. WWA called it virtually impossible without anthropogenic warming — the rare attribution claim that's both statistically firm and intuitively shocking.

## 2. [Trump administration moves to shut down NCAR; UCAR sues](https://earth.org/this-week-in-climate-news-march-2026-week-3/)
**Source:** Earth.Org · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: UCAR filed suit challenging the administration's decision to break up the National Center for Atmospheric Research in Boulder. NCAR is the country's flagship academic climate-research institution; closing or breaking it up materially degrades US capacity for hurricane forecasting, climate modeling, and atmospheric science publishing.

## 3. [NOAA upgrades El Niño outlook to 1-in-3 "strong" by winter 2026](https://earth.org/this-week-in-climate-news-march-2026-week-3/)
**Source:** Earth.Org · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: NOAA's revised forecast says El Niño is likely to form by summer and persist through year-end, with one-in-three odds of becoming "strong" by winter — a notable upward revision. Strong El Niño years reshape global weather: California floods, Indonesian drought, Pacific fisheries collapse, and global heat records. Markets are not yet priced for this.

## 4. [Greenpeace ordered to pay $345M in Dakota Access Pipeline verdict](https://earth.org/this-week-in-climate-news-march-2026-week-1/)
**Source:** Earth.Org · **Date:** 2026-03

Why this matters: A North Dakota district judge validated a $345M verdict against Greenpeace for its role in DAPL protests. The financial scale would functionally cripple Greenpeace USA's operations — and the precedent invites similar suits against environmental groups for protest activity, a major chilling effect on climate organizing.

## 5. [Kenya flooding kills 110+ amid intensified pre-monsoon rains](https://www.worldweatherattribution.org/2026/)
**Source:** World Weather Attribution · **Date:** 2026-03-06

Why this matters: Sustained flooding starting March 6 killed at least 110 people in Kenya, consistent with the broader pattern of intensified pre-monsoon rainfall across East Africa. The compounding effect — Kenya, Brazil's Minas Gerais (70+ deaths from March 12 landslides) — is the climate-vulnerability signal of the month.
