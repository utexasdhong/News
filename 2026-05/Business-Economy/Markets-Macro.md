# Markets & Macro — May 2026

## 1. [S&P 500 closes May at record 7,519.12 on May 26](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/25/stock-futures-today-live-updates.html)
**Source:** CNBC · **Date:** 2026-05-26

Why this matters: The broad index closed at a fresh all-time high on the final trading day of the month, with chips and AI capex names leading. April was the best month for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq since 2020, and May extended the rally — a remarkable run given the Hormuz blockade and the energy-price spike running through it.

## 2. [Fed holds at 3.5–3.75% with rare 8–4 dissent vote](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260429.htm)
**Source:** Federal Reserve (April 28–29 minutes released in May) · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: The four-dissent FOMC vote is the first since October 1992 — a real signal of internal disagreement about whether the Iran-war energy shock requires holding (or hiking) versus the labor-market softening that argues for cuts. The dissent pattern itself is more newsworthy than any single decision.

## 3. [Kevin Warsh takes over as Fed Chair on May 15](https://www.crestwoodadvisors.com/may-2026-economic-and-market-update/)
**Source:** Crestwood Advisors · **Date:** 2026-05-15

Why this matters: One of the most consequential central-bank leadership transitions in a generation, coming in the middle of an inflation re-acceleration and a deeply split FOMC. Warsh's hawkish reputation, prior Fed governorship, and personal closeness to the Trump administration make his first FOMC the most-watched in years.

## 4. [Energy shock pushes core inflation projection up to 2.7%, GDP still expanding](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/files/fomcprojtabl20260318.pdf)
**Source:** Federal Reserve / SEP · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: April–May data confirmed an inflation re-acceleration led by energy as the Hormuz blockade tightens. The Fed's March SEP had already revised 2026 core PCE up 0.1pp to 2.7% — by May, that revision looked too conservative. Strong corporate earnings have kept the equity rally alive, but the macro setup is increasingly unstable.

## 5. [IEA chief calls Iran war the "biggest energy crisis in history"](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/05/world-facing-biggest-ever-energy-crisis-and-more-top-energy-stories/)
**Source:** World Economic Forum · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: The IEA's framing matters because oil prices, fuel rationing in Europe and Asia, and a US fuel deficit are now feeding directly into core inflation, transport costs, and political stability. Every macro forecast for the back half of 2026 hinges on whether the blockade ends, freezes, or escalates.
