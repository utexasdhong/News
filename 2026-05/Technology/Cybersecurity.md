# Cybersecurity — May 2026

## 1. [ShinyHunters ransomware hits Instructure (Canvas) — 275M people, 3.65TB stolen](https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/news/2026/05/millions-of-students-personal-data-stolen-in-major-education-cyberattack)
**Source:** Malwarebytes Labs · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: The attack hit nearly 9,000 schools — Harvard, Stanford, UC Berkeley among them — exposing data on 275 million students, teachers, and staff. By volume of individuals affected, it is among the largest education-sector breaches ever recorded and will accelerate scrutiny of single-vendor lock-in across K-12 and higher ed LMS platforms.

## 2. [Qilin ransomware shuts down Covenant Health hospitals across Maine and New Hampshire](https://therecord.media/covenant-health-breach-qilin)
**Source:** The Record (Recorded Future) · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Attackers held access from May 18 onward; St. Joseph Hospital and St. Mary's Health System were forced to paper-order labs, with imaging and pharmacy services degraded. Roughly 480,000 individuals were impacted. Qilin is the same group that ravaged London hospitals — the cross-Atlantic pattern is now established and chronic.

## 3. [SharkStriker tracks dozens of May breaches as ShinyHunters and successor groups accelerate](https://sharkstriker.com/blog/may-2026-data-breaches/)
**Source:** SharkStriker · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Useful month-long aggregator showing the breach drumbeat — multiple breaches disclosed as late as May 25, including ACAM Systemautomation, Adensa Technology, Buffalo Niagara Convention Center, and several legal firms. Threat intel reports lateral movement inside victim networks dropping under 30 seconds in AI-assisted attacks.

## 4. [Healthcare biometrics, diagnoses, and bank details exposed in major breach](https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/news/2026/05/biometrics-diagnoses-and-bank-details-exposed-in-major-healthcare-breach)
**Source:** Malwarebytes Labs · **Date:** 2026-05

Why this matters: Biometric identifiers — unlike passwords — cannot be rotated. Healthcare's incremental adoption of biometric auth is now colliding with ransomware's industrial maturity, creating a new class of irreversible identity damage.
