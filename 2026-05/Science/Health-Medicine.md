# Health & Medicine — May 2026

## 1. [FDA approves Veppanu (vepdegestrant) for ESR1-mutated metastatic breast cancer](https://www.drugs.com/newdrugs.html)
**Source:** Drugs.com / FDA · **Date:** 2026-05-01

Why this matters: First-in-class oral PROTAC degrader of the estrogen receptor approved on May 1. PROTAC degraders — molecules that hijack a cell's own protein-disposal machinery — have been in development for over a decade; Veppanu is the most significant approval to validate the modality in solid tumors.

## 2. [FDA grants accelerated approval to Beqalzi (sonrotoclax) for relapsed mantle cell lymphoma](https://www.drugs.com/newdrugs.html)
**Source:** Drugs.com / FDA · **Date:** 2026-05-13

Why this matters: BCL-2 inhibitor positioned as a successor to venetoclax for a notoriously aggressive lymphoma subtype. Accelerated approval based on response rates means real-world confirmatory data over the next 18 months matters a lot.

## 3. [FDA approves Trimbow inhaler for maintenance treatment of asthma](https://www.drugs.com/newdrugs.html)
**Source:** Drugs.com / FDA · **Date:** 2026-05-18

Why this matters: Triple-combo (beclomethasone/formoterol/glycopyrrolate) inhaler crosses from COPD into the asthma indication. Expands a single-inhaler triple-therapy option to a much larger US patient pool.

## 4. [FDA blocks publication of agency-authored COVID and shingles vaccine safety studies](https://www.cidrap.umn.edu/public-health/state-us-vaccine-policy-may-14-2026)
**Source:** CIDRAP · **Date:** 2026-05-14

Why this matters: Reported by the New York Times — the FDA suppressed multiple studies whose conclusions supported vaccine safety. Combined with HHS Secretary Kennedy's $40–50M push to find evidence of vaccine-chronic-disease links, this is the most consequential US public-health-science governance story of the month.

## 5. [WHO publishes Climate-Health Action Plan 2025–2028](https://www.who.int/philippines/news/detail-global/18-05-2026-who-launches-advocacy--communications-and-partnerships-plan-on-the-health-response-to-climate-change--air-pollution-and-energy-poverty)
**Source:** WHO · **Date:** 2026-05-18

Why this matters: Operationalizes the World Health Assembly's climate-health mandate into a coordinated WHO program covering air pollution and energy poverty. Sets the framework that national health agencies — and donors — will work against through 2028.
