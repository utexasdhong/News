# Space — May 2026

## 1. [SpaceX launches biggest Starship yet on a mock-Starlink test flight](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/23/nx-s1-5832402/spacex-biggest-starship-flight)
**Source:** NPR · **Date:** 2026-05-22

Why this matters: The upgraded Starship carried 20 mock Starlink satellites released midflight and completed a near-orbital arc to splashdown in the Indian Ocean before erupting in flames on impact. NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman attended in person, calling it "one step closer to the Moon" — Starship remains NASA's only credible Artemis lander path.

## 2. [JWST maps the cosmic web of 164,000 galaxies — clearest yet](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260511213136.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily · **Date:** 2026-05-11

Why this matters: The COSMOS-Web survey traced filaments connecting galaxies back to when the universe was just a billion years old. The map is precise enough to test cosmological models of structure formation in ways previous surveys couldn't.

## 3. [JWST finds a massive early-universe galaxy with no rotation](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260506225135.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily / Nature Astronomy · **Date:** 2026-05-04

Why this matters: A galaxy formed less than 2 billion years after the Big Bang showing zero net spin — a property usually exclusive to old, fully-evolved galaxies near us. Forces theorists to rethink how and when angular momentum gets baked into galactic discs.

## 4. [ESA and CAS launch joint SMILE heliophysics mission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_in_spaceflight)
**Source:** Wikipedia (2026 in spaceflight) · **Date:** 2026-05-19

Why this matters: SMILE will image the solar wind–magnetosphere interaction globally for the first time, using soft X-ray and UV instruments. A rare China-Europe collaboration that survived the geopolitical chill — and a milestone for space-weather forecasting.

## 5. [SpaceX CRS-34 delivers 6,500 lb of science cargo to the ISS](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-science-cargo-launch-on-34th-spacex-resupply-mission-to-station/)
**Source:** NASA · **Date:** 2026-05-15

Why this matters: Routine, but includes the STORIE instrument install and a heavy science payload. With Starship still in test and Crew Dragon resupply still mainstream, May was a reminder of how reliant ISS science remains on Falcon 9.
